# AI What's New Collector

## Summary
Collect new and useful AI developments from multiple content channels, then organize them into an internal research feed focused on cutting-edge capabilities, real-world usage, cost reduction, and new monetization opportunities. The system should emphasize high-probability opportunities rather than novelty alone.

## Problem
AI information is scattered across podcasts, YouTube, social media, research, releases, and open-source channels, making it hard to consistently track what is new, how people are using it, how costs are changing, and where new money-making opportunities are emerging. It is also difficult to distinguish interesting signals from information that has a high probability of producing useful business or technical outcomes.

## Proposed Automation
Create a workflow that gathers updates from selected AI information sources, filters for relevance, and organizes them into a structured internal research summary. The workflow should emphasize:
- what is newly possible
- how practitioners are applying it
- how to reduce AI costs
- where new monetization opportunities may exist
- which ideas have a high probability of success
- cross-referenced evidence across multiple sources

The system should balance both business and technical signals because both can create long-term advantage.

## Questions / Unknowns
- What is the ideal output format: notes folder, weekly digest, dashboard, or searchable database?
- How often should the workflow run: daily, several times per day, or weekly with higher curation?
- Which source categories should get the highest priority: model labs, open source, creator commentary, startup/product launches, or research papers?
- What rules should define “high probability of success” for a signal or opportunity?
- What kind of cross-referencing is most useful: repeated mention across sources, technical validation plus business validation, or evidence of adoption plus economics?
